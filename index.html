My Favourite People

Shreyas Iyer

watching Shreyas Iyer on the field feels like witnessing controlled chaos a blend of finesse, aggression, and unshakable resolve. His journey from Mumbai’s maidans to international stadiums is nothing short of inspiring. He’s not just a cricketer; he’s an artist painting with boundaries and sixes, yet backed by deep discipline and determination. Whether he’s anchoring the innings or accelerating the game in crunch moments, Shreyas exudes confidence that lifts the spirits of fans across the country. His composure under pressure is remarkable like when he bounced back from injury and staked his claim with match-winning knocks. It's that hunger, that fire, that proves he's not here to follow paths he’s carving his own. From captaining Delhi in the IPL to commanding respect in India’s middle order, he has become a symbol of resilience. His stylish strokeplay and sharp cricketing mind make him a dynamic force in any format. As a fan, it’s hard not to admire his journey. It's a reminder that greatness isn’t handed over it’s earned, slowly and tirelessly, with sweat, setbacks, and staying power. "Let your setbacks be setups for your comeback. If you believe in yourself, no injury, no doubt, no challenge can hold you back. Just like Shreyas, rise every time, swing hard, and let the world know you’re not done yet."

Ram Charan Konidala

Ram Charan Konidala is not just a star he’s a phenomenon. With roots grounded in a legendary film family and wings that soar through pan-Indian success, Ram Charan has carved his own legacy marked by power, passion, and reinvention. Born into the illustrious Konidala clan, he didn’t ride on reputation alone he forged his own path with relentless effort and electrifying screen presence. His debut in Chirutha showcased raw potential, but it was Magadheera that ignited his rise a film that redefined action and fantasy in Telugu cinema. From there, he shifted gears and genres with fearless adaptability, whether it was the intense performance in Rangasthalam or his thunderous role in the global hit RRR. Every portrayal carries a signature depth where emotion meets intensity. But beyond the action packed frames and box office triumphs lies a man of discipline, humility, and deep respect for craft. He’s equally at home on the sets as he is in the boardroom, building his production house Konidela Productions and supporting bold, meaningful cinema. His charisma transcends language barriers, and his rise as a pan-Indian star is a testament to how talent, when fueled by vision, breaks boundaries. Off-screen, Ram Charan is admired for his grace, grounded nature, and philanthropic efforts.

Sudha Murthy

Sudha Murthy is the kind of person who doesn’t just change lives she uplifts them, one act of kindness and one story at a time. Engineer, philanthropist, author, and tireless changemaker, she’s a symbol of what happens when intellect meets compassion. Born in 1950 in Shiggaon, Karnataka, Sudha Murthy shattered norms early by becoming the first woman engineer hired by TELCO. It wasn’t easy in a time when engineering was seen as a man’s domain but her determination and courage proved unstoppable. She believed in merit over prejudice, and made that belief felt through her career and writing. As the chairperson of the Infosys Foundation, she’s helped transform countless rural and underserved communities building libraries, hospitals, schools, and dreams. Her approach is always hands on, humble, and deeply personal. She doesn’t seek praise she seeks impact. Her books, written in simple yet powerful language, have touched millions. Whether it's Wise and Otherwise, The Day I Stopped Drinking Milk, or her tales for children, every story carries a nugget of truth, empathy, and gentle guidance. She teaches us that strength doesn’t have to shout—it can whisper and still shake the world.


Samantha Ruth Prabhu

Samantha Ruth Prabhu is more than a celebrated actress she’s a force of nature. Born in Chennai in 1987 to a Telugu father and Malayali mother, Samantha’s journey from modeling to becoming one of South India’s most beloved stars is a testament to her talent and tenacity. Her debut in Ye Maaya Chesave (2010) was a revelation, earning her the Filmfare Award for Best Female Debut. From there, she lit up screens in hits like Eega, Rangasthalam, Mahanati, and Oh! Baby, showcasing her versatility across genres. Whether playing a fierce rebel in The Family Man or a vulnerable soul in Majili, Samantha brings depth and authenticity to every role. But her impact goes far beyond cinema. She’s a philanthropist, founding Pratyusha Support to provide medical aid to women and children. She’s also a fashion entrepreneur, co-founding the label Saaki, and recently launched her production house Tra-La-La Moving Pictures. Her commitment to handloom and sustainability earned her the title of Telangana’s handloom ambassador. In 2022, Samantha revealed her battle with myositis, an autoimmune condition. Her openness inspired millions, proving that vulnerability is a form of courage. She continues to work, heal, and uplift others—never letting setbacks define her.


Virat Kohli

Virat Kohli is not just a cricketer he’s a revolution in motion. Born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, Kohli’s journey from a passionate young boy to one of the greatest batsmen in cricket history is a tale of grit, discipline, and unmatched hunger for excellence. He burst onto the scene by leading India to victory in the 2008 U-19 World Cup, and soon made his international debut. Over the years, Kohli has redefined batting across formats, becoming the fastest to reach multiple milestones in ODIs and Tests. His aggressive style, sharp technique, and mental toughness have earned him nicknames like “King Kohli” and “The Run Machine.” As captain, he transformed India into a dominant force, especially in Test cricket, where his leadership led to historic overseas wins. His fitness first approach revolutionized team culture, inspiring a generation to prioritize athleticism and discipline. Kohli’s records are staggering: over 80 international centuries, most ODI centuries, and the highest run scorer in IPL history. He’s the only player to earn 900+ rating points in all three formats. His performances in World Cups and ICC tournaments have been clutch, often carrying the team in high-pressure moments. Off the field, Kohli is a philanthropist through the Virat Kohli Foundation, a style icon, and a devoted family man. Married to actress Anushka Sharma, he balances fame with humility and purpose.
